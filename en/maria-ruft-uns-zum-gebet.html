Mary Calls Us to Prayer

Mary Calls Us to Prayer

O Mary, conceived without sin,
 pray for us who have recourse to thee,
and for all who do not have recourse to thee,
especially for Freemasons.
(St. Maximilian M. Kolbe)

Daily Offering

Eternal Father, through the purest hands of Mary, I place in the chalice
that every priest elevates on the altar today,
my thoughts, my work, my labours and sufferings,
my present, my past, my future,
all my sins, all my loved ones, all souls in the world,
the Poor Souls, death itself.
I offer You
every beat of my heart, every breath, every step,
every sound with the intention
that each of these my actions be transformed by You
into so many acts of love in reparation for the sins of the world.
Grant that in union with Your Son Jesus Christ
my day may be a continual Mass, a constant sacrifice,
an eternal communion,
to make satisfaction to Your divine justice!
Amen.

 United with Jesus, who daily renews His total consecration in the perfect praise-, thanksgiving-, reparation- and petition-offering of the Holy Mass, we too may offer ourselves and all those entrusted to us to the Heavenly Father at the Holy Sacrifice of the Mass. Every praise, every petition and every offering are like a vessel with which we continually draw new graces from the infinite treasures of the Heart of God.

The Golden Arrow

May the most high, most adorable and ineffable Name of God be forever praised, blessed, loved, adored and glorified in heaven, on earth and under the earth, by all creatures that have come forth from the hand of God, and through the Most Sacred Heart of Jesus in the Most Blessed Sacrament of the Altar. Amen.

The divine Redeemer once said to the devout Sr. Mary of St. Peter: "Blasphemy is a poisoned arrow that wounds My Heart. I will give you a Golden Arrow that makes reparation for the profanation and blasphemy of the Most Holy Name of God." After Sister Mary received this prayer, she had a divine vision in which she saw the Most Sacred Heart of Jesus tenderly wounded by this Golden Arrow. Streams of grace burst forth from Him for the conversion of sinners. Use of the prayer: Prayed three times in honour of the Most Holy Trinity; partial indulgence, April 15, 1881.

And when we see or hear evil said or done or God blasphemed, let us say good and do good and praise God, who is blessed for all eternity.
 (St. Francis)

Special Petitions to the Holy Spirit

O Holy Spirit, I humbly and earnestly ask You,
grant me a very special grace
that I beg from Your infinite mercy.
Grant that before my death I may repair all loss of grace
which I have had the misfortune to suffer.
Grant that I may attain the highest degree of grace, merit and glory,
to which You first intended to lead me,
but which I have until now frustrated by my constant infidelity.
Finally, forgive me the sins which I have helped occasion in others
and crown Your mercy towards me by
Yourself repairing in souls the losses of grace
which they have suffered through my fault.
Amen.

Daily prayer of Fr. Beda Ludwig OSB. Imprimatur: Friburgi Helv. 4.2.1950.

A Painful Thorn

A very great and painful thorn in the Immaculate Heart of Mary is the cruel sect of Freemasonry, founded in London in 1717. Wherever it sets foot, it thrusts itself into all levels of society and all institutions of the state, in order finally to subject everything to its satanic spirit.

One of the most refined ways of its fight against its arch-enemy – the Catholic Church – consists in infiltrating the Church, by procuring "friends" up to the highest ecclesiastical dignitaries, who then become servants of Lucifer as wolves in sheep's clothing.

Pope Pius VIII calls it a satanic sect which has the demon as its god. Twenty years after its foundation the Church condemned this anti-religious association with the highest ecclesiastical penalty of excommunication.

The essence of Freemason religion consists in the perversion, in the inversion of the order of creation given by God, by turning all theological and moral foundations of the Catholic faith into their opposite. With this attitude it is at the forefront in promoting abortion, same-sex "marriage" and euthanasia, making it the perfect anti-church.

Cf. The True Face of Freemasonry, Bishop Athanasius Schneider in a lecture for the TV series Topic of the Pontifical Foundation Aid to the Church in Need, 2017

Prayer for Freemasons

Lord Jesus Christ,
You manifest Your almighty power most when You spare and have mercy.
You have said:
"Pray for those who persecute and slander you."
 We implore the mercy of Your most Sacred Heart for the souls of Freemasons,
who, deceived by the crafty wiles of the evil enemy,
are on the path of eternal perdition.
Do not let the Church, Your Bride, be oppressed by them any longer,
but grant them through the intercession of the most blessed Virgin Mary and all the saints
Your infinite mercy.
Lead them back to You,
that they may bring consolation to the Church through true repentance,
make reparation for their misdeeds
and thus attain eternal salvation.
Amen.

A Freemason is the most unfree person, whose eternal salvation is most endangered. May a movement arise in the Church to save the souls of our Freemason brethren, above all through the prayer of the Rosary and devotion to the Immaculate Heart of Mary. Her Immaculate Heart will triumph, also over Freemasonry and communism, and through Mary God will grant humanity and the Church a time of peace.

Total Consecration to Mary

O Immaculata, Queen of heaven and earth,
Refuge of sinners and our Mother,
who love us so much and to whom God has entrusted the whole order of mercy!
I, N., an unfaithful sinner,
throw myself at Your feet and beg You from the depths of my heart:
Deign to take me completely as Your property and possession.
Do with me as You please, with all the faculties of my soul and body,
with my whole life, my death and my eternity.
Dispose of me entirely as You will,
that what is said of You may be fulfilled:
"She shall crush the serpent's head."
 and likewise:
 "You alone have conquered all heresies in the whole world."
 Make me an instrument in Your hands to serve You,
to increase Your glory as much as possible
in so many fallen and lukewarm souls.
So the gentle kingdom
of the Most Sacred Heart of Jesus may spread ever more.
For wherever You enter, You obtain the grace of conversion and sanctification,
for all graces reach us from the Most Sacred Heart of Jesus only through Your hands.
Grant that I may praise You, O most holy Virgin,
and give me power against Your enemies.
Amen.
 (St. Maximilian M. Kolbe)

To the Queen of the Holy Angels

August Queen of heaven and sovereign Lady of the angels,
You have received from God from the beginning the power and mission
to crush the head of Satan.
Therefore we humbly beseech You,
send the heavenly legions to our aid,
that under Your leadership they may pursue the demons,
fight them everywhere, repel their insolent attacks
and cast them back into the abyss.
"Who is like God?"
 O good and gentle Mother,
You will always be our love and our hope!
O Mother of God, send us the holy angels,
that they may defend us and keep the evil enemy away from us.
O holy angels and archangels, defend and protect us!
Amen.

On January 13, 1864, the founder of the Congregation of the Handmaids of Mary, Fr. Cestac (1801–1868), was in the Sanctuary of Our Lady of Refuge in Anglet (Pyrenees) suddenly struck as by a ray of divine clarity. He saw the demons spread over the whole world and wreak unspeakable harm everywhere. At the same time he had a vision of the most blessed Virgin Mary, who told him that the demons were indeed let loose upon the whole world. Therefore the hour had come to invoke her as Queen of Angels and to ask her to send legions of holy angels to fight the powers of the underworld. "My Mother," said the priest, "You are so good, could You not send them without anyone asking?" – "No," answered the most blessed Virgin, "prayer is a condition set by God, even when one wishes to receive graces." – "If that is so, my Mother, would You then teach us how we must pray?" Thereupon he received from the most blessed Virgin the prayer "August Queen," and spread it throughout the world after episcopal approval. This prayer, accompanied by extraordinary graces, was recommended by Pope Pius IX and enriched by Leo XIII and Pius X with the granting of a plenary indulgence.
